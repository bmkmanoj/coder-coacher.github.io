Motorola Moto G 2nd Generation / G2 Unboxing and Review

2014-09-12
What’s up internet? I Manish from rebellionrider.com And today I am going to do the unboxing of brand new Motorola Moto g2 or Motorola Moto g second generation which is a budget smartphone recently launched by Motorola. But before moving ahead a hearty thanks to dear friend who let me unbox and review this brand new Motorola moto g2 on my YouTube channel. This phone is exclusively available on Flipkart. And costs only 12999 Indian Rupees which is approx. 215 us dollars So let’s start the unboxing. Unlike other budget phones in the same category Motorola keeps the box of moto g2 very minimal. Let’s open the box and see what’s inside it? First thing which comes in front of you as soon as you open this box is the phone itself. Motorola has launched Moto g2 in 2 different colors white and black and we have a white color variant here for the unboxing. Ok apart from the phone you will also get – a travel charger and head sets and some manuals. This box doesn’t include any USB connecting cables. Though the price of the phone is very low but the phone itself doesn’t feel cheap at all. When you hold the phone for the first time in your hand you will feel that this phone screams quality. At the front of Moto g2 we have 5 inches IPS LCD screen with Gorilla Glass 3 protection, This is a 720p HD screen with 720 x 1280 pixels resolution and approx. 294 Pixel density which means good color depth on the display. And we also get 2MP front camera along with 2 audio speakers. You must have already seen this kind of front speaker with Motorola Moto E. At the back of the phone we have 8 mega Pixel autofocus Camera with LED flash. This camera is capable of recording 720p HD videos on 30frame per second with stereo sound. Moto g2 has packed several interesting features with this camera such as Auto Focus, Slow Motion Video, Burst Mode, Auto HDR, Panorama and Tap to Focus. The material of the back cover of moto g2 is smooth when you touch it and you don’t get any fingerprints. And of course how can I forget to mention the most talked about dimple with Motorola logo on it. On the right side of the phone we have power button and the volume lockers whereas left side of Moto G2 has nothing. On the top of the phone we have 3.5 mm audio jack for headset along with the noise cancellation microphone. At the bottom we have Micro-USB charging port. Under the battery cover you will get a 2070mAH un-detachable battery. We also have 2 SIM card slots for micro SIMs both of which support 3G GSM network. But you can only use 3g Network on one SIM at a time and not on both the SIMs together. This phone comes with 16GB internal storage and unlike the previous Motorola Moto G this time with Moto G2 we have a Micro SD slot using which you can extend your memory up to 32 GBs. This phone has Qualcomm Snapdragon 400 processor with 1.2 GHz quad-core CPU along with Adreno 305 GPU and 1GB RAM. Now let’s talk about the sensors in Motorola moto g2. This phone comes with proximity, ambient light and the accelerometer sensor. Proximity sensor which turns off the display when you are on a call, the accelerometer controls the orientation of the phone and helps with motion gaming. The ambient light sensor adjusts the screen's brightness for optimal viewing in different conditions All together these high end specifications give you a very smooth Moto G2 experience in a pocket friendly price. Verdicts Though the phone will give a premium quality feel yet the head phones which comes packed with the phone seems quite minimal with below average sound quality. With the previous Moto G, users faced Muffled voice speaker problem during call. But with this second generation Moto G I haven’t faced any such problem. If you ask me about the review then I will go with 9 out of 10 as the headphones put a bit of a damper on the rest of the excellent features. I would also like to give you all a heads up for the charger – it has very less juice of just 0.5 ampere which may lead to your phone taking extensive time to get charged. If you want quick charging then better buy a universal charger of 1.5 ampere or more. Apart from that, Motorola Moto G2 is a great budget phone and worth buying. That’s all guys.
