Self-XSS: Are You Safe From Yourself?

2015-05-04
Self Cross-Site Scripting attacks are becoming a very large issue recently, especially for users of the website Facebook. So what are self cross-site scripting attacks? and how can you protect yourself from them? Well self cross-site scripting or self XSS for short is a social engineering attack which tricks a victim into running a malicious script on themself. In laymans terms a "Scam". Usually the scam is in the form of "Follow these 3 easy steps to hack any Facebook account!" Step 1. Go to the victims profile Step 2. Right click and click on "Inspect Element" and then click the console tab. Step 3. Paste the code into the box at the bottom and press enter. You can find the code at this website. Wait 5 seconds! and Done! Good Luck! Now self XSS isn't a new threat, I haven't been able to find reputable statistics, however in the last 12 months Facebook and Google+ have been trying very hard to combat this social engineering attack. Most commonly this attack is targeting facebook accounts. This attack has become so common that web browsers like firefox and chrome now display warnings about self-xss attacks. Facebook also has a warning that is visible when the user opens the developer console. The issue with this attack is that the victims are so desperate for this supposed hack to work that they ignore warnings and still fall victim to the attacks. Because this is becoming such a major issue, education of the attack is very important, especially if your business uses facebook as a method of marketing. Having your business facebook account comprimised will not look good. Facebook has some very useful articles on what a self-xss attack may look like. As well as what self-xss is.
