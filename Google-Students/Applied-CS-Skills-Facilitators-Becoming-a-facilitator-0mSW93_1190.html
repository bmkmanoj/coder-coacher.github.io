planning and includes things like arranging a suitable space and time to meet in marketing the program to find students and choosing the program content and length applied CS units have two parts the independent groundwork and the workshop groundwork is completed by student participants in their own time and is important review and preparation for the workshop groundwork consists of reading watching videos and occasionally practicing problems workshops are the in-person sessions facilitated by you workshops are interactive and used the concepts reviewed during the groundwork to build an Android game in small groups between both of these parts students will complete one or more activities and cover concepts from computer science and Android development as a facilitator you will need a good understanding of the concepts being taught as well as experience coding in Java so that you can effectively help your students during the workshops having planned marketed and scheduled an applied CS program facilitators also manage communications and set expectations with students arrive early to the workshop space to make sure everything is ready introduce the workshop by briefly explaining the CS concepts covered by the unit and the Android activity students will build focus on facilitation rather than lecturing ideally workshop time is best spent coding troubleshooting bugs and working collaboratively register workshop attendants make themselves available for student questions Google provides facilitators with lots of guidance and resources to support their journey from suggested program formats to detailed guides and concept videos for each unit these resources have been built and refined by the experiences of hundreds of other facilitators and programs that have
