Sprout by HP Developer Tutorial: 3D Capture

2016-01-15
in this tutorial I will show you how to capture 3d objects with the sprout one of the features of the sprout is its ability to capture real-world objects not only in two dimensions like a normal camera but also in three dimensions we can capture three dimensional objects with the 3d capture app which comes built into the sprout in this tutorial we will be using an HP 3d capture stage to assist in capturing the object while not required the stage makes the process more automated and easier every time you use the capture stage you will have to scan the background before you scan your object this is because the stage is not fixed and we don't want our end model to have the stage as a part of it the black and white lines that you see being projected onto the mat are part of the method that sprout uses to understand 3d space you can set the capture stage to be flat or tilted at an angle as benefits the object you are scanning in this tutorial we will be doing a flat scan you can easily change the settings of each capture to allow you to do a greater or fewer number of passes the more you do the more data your program will have and the more accurate your model will be in this video I will be doing only one pass and that pass is divided into six segments each time the stage moves to the next segment a new moment is captured and the 3d information is saved at the end of the process the application will stitch all of this data together to form one model as you can see there may be a couple of holes in the model due to us only doing one pass for this example you can choose to leave these sort of gaps if they are intentional or if you wish to repair them yourself alternatively you may choose to have this software close them automatically this model turned out really well for only doing one pass typically you may need to do two three or even more depending on the complexity of the object once the model is fused you can use the 3d viewer to manipulate it to a certain extent changing its material its color or exporting it to a different format allowing it to be edited by another 3d modeling software before saving it once you are satisfied with the model you can save it and it will appear in your 3d capture models folder a primary use of this would be to scan a real world object that you own make tweaks to it as a model and then print it out using a 3d printer this video covered capturing 3d objects with the sprout
