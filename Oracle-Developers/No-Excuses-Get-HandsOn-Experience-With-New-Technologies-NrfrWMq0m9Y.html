No Excuses: Get Hands-On Experience With New Technologies

2017-11-02
As I'm walking down the streets of San Francisco here at Oracle Openworld 2017 my tech tip today is actually quite simple: there's no excuse for not getting hands-on experience with new technologies. The only way to learn is by sitting down and actually trying things out, as I'm about to do when I go to the hands-on lab on Oracle Functions, the Oracle solution for serverless. I've read about serverless, I've read a book about Amazon Lambda, I've been having conversations about serverless. Really the only way to actually find out what it is and how you can make use of it is we're trying it out yourself Of course it's easy to try things out when you're at a conference like this one where people organize hands-on labs and people who have created that kind of technology are here to explain it to you and guide you through the workshop. However, for today's developer there are so many opportunities to get code, to download environments, to start playing for free. Even on cloud environments you don't need to bring in a powerful laptop. There's no better advice I can give you than to sit down, download the environments, start up your Docker container, fire up VirtualBox to start virtual machine, and try even the simplest things out for yourself. Through configuration as code, people share [unintelligible] files that will start up complex Kubernetes clusters with all kinds of Docker containers. The only thing you have to do is to start it from a command line, which may be big step for people used to IDEs. Before you know it a complex environment has spun up, and allows you to start interacting, even if it's nothing more than just a simple hello world example. I've arrived now at the hands-on lab. You can see the set-up, all the machines waiting for people to get their hands dirty and their feet wet. "Certainly would be worth a try!"
