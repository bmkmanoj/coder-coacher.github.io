Stroke Recovery with Kinect

2014-01-22
Microsoft Research Asia and Seoul National University have created a research project called stroke recovery with Kinect it's a cost-effective interactive home rehabilitation system promoter recovery after a stroke and was built using the Microsoft Kinect for Windows software development kit stroke recovery with Connect uses the Kinect sensors three-dimensional camera to capture skeletal movements of the patient performing the therapy the system interprets the data to measure and evaluate a patient's progress in three game like exercises the classic box and block test exercise evaluates patients coordination gross manual dexterity and motor skills as they virtually attempt to pick up blocks one by one and put them into a box in a set amount of time the program displays patients scores providing immediate reinforcement of a patient's progression the second exercise challenges the patient to assume a target pose displayed on the computer monitor and then duplicate the targets position as it moves the patient receives what is known as a fugle mayer assessment score based on his or her success the final program is an outer space game that enables patients to exercise their reflex and reaction abilities as they guide a spaceship through space while attempting to avoid oncoming asteroids the application tracks the stroke patients hand trajectory and notes variations in movement of the elbow and shoulder
