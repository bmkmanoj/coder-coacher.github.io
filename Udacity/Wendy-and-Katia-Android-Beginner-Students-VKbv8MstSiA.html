Wendy and Katia, Android Beginner Students

2016-06-21
My name is Wendy Bravo. I'm a licensed psychotherapist. This is Katia, my daughter. She's 11 years old. If you want to learn Android, definitely take the Android Development for Beginners class first. They teach you really clearly, and for those who have no idea about programming, they take it step by step through. The class really breaks down, from the beginning, every single thing that you need to know to keep advancing. And once you start doing it, and then you get to the point where you actually see what you've been writing running on your phone, it's like, "wow!". There's this Just Java class, where it's like a coffee-ordering app. I decided to take it a little further, add some more stuff I want to add with my newfound knowledge. The other piece I really like is how they talk about making errors, how it's part of the programming. But the class really makes you experience it. And I think for me that's the biggest difference. When you get to experience it and you really have that little tension... "OK, where is it? How am I going to fix this thing?" Once you actually fix it, the more you struggle... you feel even better then. The instructors make it really clear and make it fun to learn, at the same time. Overall, the experience was really great. If I started and learned after so many years in a completely different career that had nothing to do with it, being intimidated, having all these erroneous beliefs about it, if even with all that I was able to learn, I think anyone can do it.
