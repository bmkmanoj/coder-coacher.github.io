why do we care about missing data let's talk through a couple of points as to why we should care about missing data before we complete an analysis using that data one of the more basic reasons we might care about missing data is that some statistical algorithms just won't work if there are values missing from the data obviously we can't move forward with an analysis if the process we're using won't get us to the end result so in those cases we just have to deal with the missing data a second more subtle reason is that missing items can add bias to a model now the term bias and statistics refers to the tendency of an analysis to either over or under estimate the values of that specific field or parameter in other words if bias is present it could push the model results higher or lower depending on how much bias there is in one of the variables we're using for example in our hypothetical survey where one of the questions is related to age many older respondents might be hesitant to give their age and if we could actually know what the real ages of the respondents are then we would have a more accurate picture and one of the facts we would find is that the average age of the respondents is really 44 and a half but without the real data there we would be working with an average age of 40 depending what our business question and analysis was trying to solve we would be creating downward bias that is biased towards a younger demographic in our analytic model this could affect our results and more importantly the business decisions could be made on inaccurate modeling as a side note if you'd like more information on the various ways a model process can be affected by bias take a look at these links
