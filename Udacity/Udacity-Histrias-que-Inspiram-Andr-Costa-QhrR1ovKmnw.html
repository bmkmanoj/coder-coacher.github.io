Udacity - Histórias que Inspiram - André Costa

2017-12-07
Data Science is a 21st-century job. Data just keeps coming. There's more and more of it, and we need more people to process it and draw conclusions. My name is André Costa and I work with Business Intelligence at Gympass. Originally I grew up in Brasília. I've played a lot of videogames as a kid. I've always loved technology and dreamed of being a programmer to make my own videogames. I have a degree in Business and I'm getting my 6th Udacity Nanodegree. I started studying Introduction to Programming to get a firm grasp, and then I took the Data Analysis and the Machine Learning courses. Now I'm also taking the course on Artificial Intelligence in order to have a good grasp of these subjects. I came to São Paulo after I was offered a spot in the Sales Department of Gympass. Later I had the chance to move over to BI, because there was an open spot. I took it. I had the knowledge, I had worked with that. I thought: "Why not go for it?" I changed departments a few weeks later. I've worked in BI ever since. Gympass is still a very new company. That is why we experiment a lot and that's why we need Data Science, to understand what's going on in each experiment and draw conclusions about the best way to act in each of the new things we try, whether it is an expansion in a new country, or a new department we'll create, or a new kind of contract we'll do. I've made projects for Gympass where my Udacity courses were of great help. For instance, I built a dashboard that used several tables. In order to gather all this information in one place the Udacity course on databases served me well. I also did a client clustering analysis for different client profiles. This clustering model building I learned at Udacity. For my career I want to work at the boundary between Tech and Business to try and unite these two worlds, draw useful conclusions and with Data Science, to move the world forward.
