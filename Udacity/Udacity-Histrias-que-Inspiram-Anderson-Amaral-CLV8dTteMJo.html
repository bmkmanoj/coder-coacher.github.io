Udacity - Histórias que Inspiram - Anderson Amaral

2017-10-11
Machine learning can change people's lives through medical diagnosis. Machine learning can improve traffic. I've built models even to find out who had scored more goals in soccer championships. My name is Anderson Luis Amaral. I'm currently the CDO - Chief Data Officer - at Dataholics. I work as the company's data scientist. I also build models using machine learning. I'm from Pindamonhangaba, in São Paulo's countryside. I was raised both there and in the capital. I didn't know which career to pursue when I was young. I always enjoyed looking at data. I was a very curious kid. I had to find a way of channeling that. I worked with technology, IT, and software development, but there was still something missing. I have no problem admitting I was only an average developer. I wasn't extraordinary. I wouldn't even consider myself a senior developer, far from it. I took an interest in machine learning, but I knew little about it. I didn't know where to start. I started studying on my own and signed up for the Udacity programs. As soon as I entered the field of data science, I saw a huge difference, as I became highly sought-after. I was truly impressed. It was huge. I was getting job offers every single day. When I met the people at Dataholics, they had been operating as a data mining company. They would perform data mining, but would not build any models based on those data. So we sat down and came up with the machine learning services that are now the company's main product. When Anderson joined our team, not only did we go from a company that provided data to one that provided intelligence, but we also underwent big changes. He actually implemented this machine-learning technology. That's why I'm a partner now. He started behind the scenes and took center stage. That's what really happened to him. For the last 4 months, we've won, on average, one prize per month. Demand for our products is so high at the moment that we're struggling to meet it. If there was anything I could change, I probably would've studied machine learning earlier and would've put it into practice in my previous jobs, because you see surprising results very quickly and you have answers that only data could give you.
