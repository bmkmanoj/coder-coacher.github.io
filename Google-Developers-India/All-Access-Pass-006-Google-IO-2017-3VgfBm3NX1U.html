[MUSIC PLAYING] MANORANJAN PADHY: Hey, everyone. I'm Manoranjan Padhy, and I'm at the Google I/O 2017. Right now I have Ankur Kotwal with me. Ankur is the developer advocate at Google. Hey, Ankur. How are you? ANKUR KOTWAL: Great. How are you? MANORANJAN PADHY: I'm doing good. So I'm going to have a few questions for you. One of them are like-- there are a lot of developers in India developing Android apps. Do you have a message for them, for how they can be successful in developing these apps? ANKUR KOTWAL: I think being successful in app development has to do with building a great app. So have a great idea, execute on it well, and then consider some very specific technical challenges that you might face in India. So you mentioned Indian developers. I'd probably say there's three things that developers could consider. The first one is your APK size. Keep your APK size as small as possible, because it makes it very easy for users to get your apps. The smaller it is, the faster it's going to download and install. The second I'd say is actually network conditions. Be resilient to low quality networks or intermittent networks. If you can make your app independent of the network quality, you'll have a great experience whether you have a fantastic network or whether you have a weak network. And the third one I'd say is actually memory usage or RAM, because when you start to look at vast amounts of users, they tend to have some low cost devices, and often those low cost devices have less RAM. So having your app work well in low memory conditions is going to treat you well, whether you've got a low memory device or a device with lots and lots of memory. MANORANJAN PADHY: How did you come up with this three things in the list or this list itself? ANKUR KOTWAL: So this list is actually part of the building for billions framework, where we actually list a number of things that app developers can do to reach billions of users worldwide. And that list, we actually came up with it talking to developers, including developers in India. They told us, it was you guys that told us how you're optimizing your apps. And then what we did is we looked to formalize those, and then validate them, and then publish it. So developers.android.com/billions. MANORANJAN PADHY: Earlier in the keynote today, [INAUDIBLE] was talking about Android Go. So do you have anything to say about it or talk about it? ANKUR KOTWAL: I'm really excited about Android Go, because what we're going to do is make a lighter version of the Android operating system, ship it with lighter apps, and provide a Play Store that highlights apps that work great on Android Go devices. So the building for billions framework is perfect, because if you can adhere to that framework, you're really well-positioned to work well on an Android Go device. MANORANJAN PADHY: Thanks, Ankur, for taking time for us. And hope you have a good I/O ahead. ANKUR KOTWAL: You're welcome. Thank you. [MUSIC PLAYING]
