CppCon 2017: Jens Weller “A C++ review Community”

2017-10-30
Hi, so you might have heard about the review community we launched around August, which is focused on reviewing C++ libraries, and I decided to talk about this shortly. And so this started with a Tweet by Eric in last year by October, he was kind of asking why don't we have such a community or why doesn't something like this exist. It would be cool to have that, and I was thinking about something similar for awhile. And but what really got me started was, C++ Norway I gave a talk on comparing the Boost and the Qt communities and also this got me kind of started into thinking what would we need today as a community, or what would we want to have today as a community. And so this kind of manifests then as a Subreddit which is now reality, it's under cpp review, r/cpp_review on a Reddit and there's gonna be an upcoming list at Meeting C++ at my website of the libraries which actually passed this review. And so the the idea about reviews, I was thinking about how should we review, so I would like to have, of course we focus on one library and then there's one Reddit thread for that which is kind of used as a forum, and there's discussions et cetera. But just, you know what should a review really be. If you look at Boost, we have a review manager and you are supposed to invest some time into it, we have a high expectation on such a review and it's high volume, it's like 600 e-mails and I don't know if e-mails are still the best medium to do reviews. And awhile ago Robert Ramey did a great initiative by setting up the Boost Library Incubator. But you have some various, here you can, most of us already have Reddit account but this is like a local website so you have to create a local login and to a WordPress account there and also unfortunately though Robert launches a couple of years ago and presented the Boost. But I kind of, until today there's, I feel kind of a missing integration into Boost of that. So and in comparison to what I currently plan as I don't want r/cpp_review to be as focused and as strict as Boost reviews are. So if you don't have such a high quality standard as Boost and we aim more at providing a different view on a library. So from Boost coming I see like a lot of what we do in reviews, for this conference or this like often experts only, when we have like this expert cult in C++. Everyone wants to be cutting edge and on the contrary I kind of think actually if we review a library we should have the contribution of everyone. It should be a group effort maybe. Because I found this great quote. In the beginner's mind there are many possibilities but in the expert's there are few. And maybe some of those possibilities which the beginners see today are things which an expert only doesn't see because he comes from a different time. So for people which are not yet experts, or want to go there or just want to have there's lots of low hanging fruit like documentation, code readability, code smell, it's just look through the code do you don't like something or think this is wrong. Scott Meyer says this, then you can ask on the Subreddit and discuss with the author and other people can contribute to that. And of course you can also do the non so low hanging fruit like you can try it yourself and if fuzzing the library, and static analyzers and leaks and lattice. There's lots of things you can do and then as this group effort, any contribution then adds to the sum and we have the exchange with the devs from the library, we can discuss it and of course you get to rant on someone else's code and you can learn. So to summarize, my approach is more like more feedback oriented, I don't want to be as strict as boost is and the r/cpp Reddit is the place to go. And I see this feedback to kind of like, come from everyone and all contributions are welcome. The review is one month and after that it stays public and people can later go and see but the responsibility is still by the library developer. Yeah so libraries just you know, the normal thing which we expect from libraries. And I expect like the library author, I want them still to have the freedom to do their choices, what they want. Like the systems, et cetera and same for developer and the community too.
