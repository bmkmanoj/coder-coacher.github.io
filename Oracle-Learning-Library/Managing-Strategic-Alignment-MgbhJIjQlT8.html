Managing Strategic Alignment

2018-01-31
use strategies to define organizational goals and initiatives then assess how projects and proposals align with those goals in this video we'll define a strategy align it to projects and then use measures to assess performance let's get started on the summary and settings page strategies are typically at the organizational business units or departments level you can also create a hierarchy of strategies to break a top-level strategy down to functional or operational level for example a business driver can be broken down into a strategic theme and then further into goals and initiatives align projects and proposals to the strategies and then assess how they meet and the strategies objectives if a project is not completely aligned with the strategy assign a percentage after projects are aligned use measures to monitor strategies performance use manual measures for strategies if the performance data is not derived from the aligned projects measures allow you to quantitatively assess the performance by comparing actual values against targets create thresholds to provide visual indicators of how projects are performing for example when monitoring the strategy for increased sales create a measure that tracks revenue on a weekly basis and indicates when revenue does not smeet or exceeds the target value use the summary scorecard page to monitor strategies using the associated measures view measures past current and future trends make adjustments as needed when the strategies change
