Understanding Risk

2018-01-31
there are two types of risk that can affect a project the first type of risk is activity uncertainty this is the general variability that makes it impossible to predict how long any activity will take to complete think of something you do on a regular basis your commute to work for instance some days it may take twenty minutes and other days it may take 40 you could estimate how long it will take within a range of uncertainty but you can never predict how long it will take exactly prime risk Andals activity uncertainty by enabling you to specify a range of values for each activities duration the second type of project risk consists of risk events external events that can disrupt or impact a project bad weather or road construction for example could make your work commute take hours prime handles risk events with a risk register the register uses probability and risk impact thresholds to quantify the potential effects of a risk on the project for example a risk might have a probability of 40 to 60% a scheduled impact of 20 to 35 days and a cost impact of 50 to 75,000 dollars when a risk analysis is run prime simulates the project thousands of times using random Monte Carlo sampling of the activity uncertainty and risk impact values activity uncertainty values are selected on every simulation while schedule and cost impact values are selected for the percentage of simulations specified by each risks probability threshold once the simulations are completed prime subjects the results to statistical analysis the distribution histogram displays a statistical breakdown for project and activity cost duration and start and finish dates the statistics table displays the probability of meeting the project's calculated finish dates in this case the calculated finish date was achieved in only 4% of the simulation outcomes the table also displays a more realistic finish date one achieved on 90% of the simulation outcomes main impact tornado charts ranked the activities and risks that have the greatest average impact on the project's duration and cost activity charts also provide a breakdown of the contribution made by each risk to duration or cost risk removal impact tornado charts ranked the risks that have the greatest impact on project cost and finished 8 these along with the mean impact tornado charts help you focus your risk management efforts on the risks and activities that have the greatest impact on the project
