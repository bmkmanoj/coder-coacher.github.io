in cloud environments the protection of data should rest with the collector or custodian which is the end user when third parties are involved the end user is at the disposal of an external protection main contract considerations here first one is due diligence once the consumer decides to migrate cloud the organization should evaluate its own policies practices and identify any barriers related to the proposed cloud migration for example the company might have policies where any change in the control of data is not allowed also companies might be required to do due diligence of cloud service provider in order to determine if the company is suitable to its internal policies and capable of protection of assets contract is the second one the consumer and service provider should enter into a written contract mostly non-negotiable which clearly enumerates the responsibilities of both parties in cases where negotiation is possible consumer has to make it without losing actual benefits example if the customer wishes to send HIPAA covered information to cloud it is mandatory to find a provider who adheres to HIPAA HIPAA is the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act which any standard healthcare provider has to adhere to the third one is monitoring testing and updating cloud is continuously evolving and both parties should actively adapt to the changes constant monitoring testing and updating are mandatory to ensure whether processes and policies are rightly followed also the legalities and compliance policies evolve with time organizations have to stay up
