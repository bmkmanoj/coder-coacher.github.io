Service Management Roles: Service Owner | ITIL V3 Foundation Training

2011-11-27
roll is a set of responsibilities activities and authorities granted to a person or a team roles are defined in the processes one person or team may have multiple roles one of the key roles in service management is that of service owner the service owner is accountable for a specific service within an organization regardless of whether the underpinning technology components processes or professional capabilities resigned service ownership is as critical to service management as establishing ownership for processes which cross multiple vertical silos or departments for example owner of a payroll service for a global MNC this service may be delivered to the employees across the globe through a website which might be hosted in data center in Ireland and supported by teams in India irrespective of the underlying technology mechanisms and geography the owner is accountable for the effective delivery of the service which is making sure that each employee of the MNC gets his or her pay on a predefined date every month the key responsibilities of service owner are first to act as prime customer contact for all service related inquiries and issues this is the person MNC employees call in case any issues with their pay second to ensure that the ongoing service delivery and support meet agreed customer requirements that is to say to ensure that everybody gets their salaries on the due date third to identify opportunities for service improvements fourth to liaise with the appropriate process owners throughout the service management life cycle fifth to ask for required data statistics and reports for analysis and to facilitate effective with monitoring and performance
