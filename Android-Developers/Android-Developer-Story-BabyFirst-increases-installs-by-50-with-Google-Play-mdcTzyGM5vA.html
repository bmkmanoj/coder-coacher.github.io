Android Developer Story: BabyFirst increases installs by 50% with Google Play

2016-02-18
[upbeat music] Sharon Rechter: My partner Guy Oranim and I started BabyFirst with the idea and a passion to create good educational content for babies, toddlers, and their parents. Today we have over 30 million downloads for BabyFirst apps. When we learned about the family category on the GooglePlay store, it was a hundred percent match to the BabyFirst values, because a BabyFirst app will always have a family value to allow a child and a parent to interact and it will always have age-appropriate content. We have seen that parents can easily find the content they are looking for. We now have 40% more downloads on GooglePlay than we do on other platforms. We like that. Yuval Rechter: The store listing experiments tool really allows us to know what works in the store, which icon works, which video works, which screenshot works. For the first time, we can actually test it with real Android users, just knowing--and 100% almost-- what actually works. And that's what we like. It's a drag-and-drop. You can allocate some of the traffic to the new experiment. So the risk is low. The benefit is high. In "ABC," for example, when we tested the icons, we tried to do a darker icon or a brighter icon. In the icon itself, different colors. We tried to do two-dimensional or three-dimensionals, and actually see what works better for the user and what is more visible. After running the experiments on the "ABC" app, we saw 140% increase in the downloads. It was great. [upbeat music] In the "Play Phone" app, we tested screenshots. We implemented an image of a phone in the screenshot itself. We played with text, with colors, with shapes. And this resulted in an increase of 11% in organic downloads. [upbeat music] Another simple thing that we have done, we have localized our store listing page in ten languages. This resulted in an increase of 150% in organic downloads outside the U.S. Through a combination of store experiments, the family category, localization, we're able to see a 50% increase in downloads across our portfolio. Sharon: I think we're super lucky. We get to work on something we love. We make kids happy every single day. Now we intend to offer even better apps for kids but also talk to the moms, because we have their trust right now and want to help make moms' life easy. We're super excited about the future, because the model works and we can't wait for it. [upbeat music]
