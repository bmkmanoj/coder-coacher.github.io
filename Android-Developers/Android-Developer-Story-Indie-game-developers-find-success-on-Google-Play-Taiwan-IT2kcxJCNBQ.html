Android Developer Story: Indie game developers find success on Google Play (Taiwan)

2018-01-23
To me, developing games Is like breathing The sun, the air, water, and games The hunger I had when I was young, wanting to accomplish something It’s a strong desire like breathing and life instincts Developing games on my own sounds quite lonely But that’s not true at all Developing a game by myself allows me to Work together with international composers or voice talents Then we can showcase our product to players from all over the world Like the previous game I developed, which was Installed 5 million times on Google Play And received 16,000 reviews I love electronic music And some geometric styles After adding some mathematics and physics into the mix Like “Magnetized”, for example It has that retro style plus a bit of triptunes Then you have “Beat Stomper”, which is a modern EDM “Push &amp; Pop” is like returning to the 80s electronic music With the addition of a 2.5D visual style Even though all three titles might sound similar to each other But actually, this is what’s most fun about developing games You need to use limited resources To explore unlimited possibilities When I was young, my younger brother and I Loved to go play all types of arcade games on the streets My favorite type of game was the spaceship shooting game We’ve been searching for this type of exciting shooting games for a long time Eventually, my two brothers and I decided to develop a game on our own This is also why our workshop is called “Uwan” At the beginning, I developed games by using the JAVA programming language At that time, there were no global platforms available So I was only able to market my games domestically Later on, we launched our very first mobile game “Lightning Fighter 2” The game was downloaded over a million times after launch And through Google Play’s Store listing experiments The installation rate for “Lightning Fighter 2” was increased to 91% in Indonesia Aside from “Lightning Fighter 2” We also designed a strategic shooter called “Lightning Rangers” A game like “Lightning Rangers” requires more server-related technologies So we decided to use Google Cloud, which was a huge help in terms of game development We also used Google Play Alpha Beta test features To help us obtain numerical feedback from players before the product is launched As well as to enhance the quality of our game Actually, game development is to touch every player’s heart through hope It is our biggest goal The idea behind OPUS comes from exploration and stories Every Saturday night, we hope players can Experience the touching stories by exploring our games It’s like having the experience of reading a great novel With a back-end service such as the Google Game Services We can update our games more quickly In order to present the best parts of our games to everyone Therefore, in terms of ARPU and conversion rate, our OPUS Earth Project Have increased by more than 200% in a very short amount of time To someone like me, who is an independent game developer It is a huge help
