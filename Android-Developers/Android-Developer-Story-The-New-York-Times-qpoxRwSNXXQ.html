Android Developer Story: The New York Times

2014-10-20
[MUSIC PLAYING] PAUL SMURL: Technology is upending our entire industry and has quickened in the last few years-- shifting the traditional media and print-based organization to one that is a global digital media organization. "The New York Times" is known for the highest quality journalism on the planet. We've been in business for over 160 years. Folks who are using our apps are more deeply engaging with the content there. They're more likely to come back frequently, go deeper, and ultimately become subscribers. KATE HARRIS: We actually debuted our Android app in 2010. So we were very early to the platform. And since then, it's just been about growth. And it's been-- over the past two years-- explosive. To build a successful Android product, it's essential to have a team that really understands the platform, that lives the platform, that's Android-obsessed. And we are very lucky to have that at "The New York Times." Android makes it really easy to build apps that are intuitive to use. It's also easy as an app developer or publisher to build apps that use our own typography, to make the app feel like "The New York Times." Before we integrated Play Billing, our subscription process took around two minutes. And we were asking our users to fill out a series of forms, all on their phone. And now with Play Billing, because the user has a credit card or their Paypal information associated with their account, the user can subscribe to "The New York Times" within two taps. Some of the tap features I really like about Play Billing was, one, we're able to enable local currency billing in over 50 countries. And, two, it was very easy to add a free trial. With Play Billing, we're able to realize a 125% increase in subscription conversion. PAUL SMURL: We did the math. We looked at whether the upside, in terms of incremental subscriptions, would more than offset the 30% that we pay to Google. And in fact, it does. Android is an incredibly important platform for us. It's one of our fastest growing platforms. We consider our investment in Android an essential one. And in terms of our international growth ambitions, Android for us has been a table stakes investment. [MUSIC PLAYING]
